01 — Diagnosis

What's Broken on the Dashboard

The dashboard today functions as a portal to scholarships and partner offers. It should function as a funding cockpit — showing students exactly how much money is flowing toward them and what to do next.

RETENTION Users Don't Come Back

The vast majority submit a few no-essay applications during onboarding and never return. The failure is the transition from signup-driven to self-directed engagement. After 30 days with no return, users are essentially lost.

VALUE Points Feel Like Nothing

The dashboard shows "0 Bold Points" with no context on what they're worth. The 3,000 BP threshold feels impossibly far for a new user. The connection between "earn points" and "pay off debt" is never made visceral or immediate.

CLARITY 329 Matches Is Overwhelming

"329 Scholarships Matched to You" is a firehose, not a recommendation. There's no signal of fit, win probability, or where to start — so most users don't start at all.

TRUST Partners Feel Like Ads

The "Vetted Partner Program" disclosure frames offers as advertising. Students don't understand that completing partner offers is how they earn toward real loan payments.

MOBILE 30–50% More Revenue Left on Table

Mobile generates significantly more revenue per session, but there's no prominent app download CTA on the web dashboard. Push notifications — the best re-engagement channel — require app installation.

DIRECTION No Clear Next Action

After landing, the user must self-direct. There's no opinionated "here's what to do right now" layer — no weekly structure, no personalized nudge, no momentum signal to drive a second session.

Every element on the dashboard should answer one question: "What should I do right now to get closer to paying off my student debt?" — Bold.org Revenue & Dashboard Strategy Guide

What Changed & Why

HERO Dollars, Not Points

"$2.00 earned toward your loans" replaces "267 BP earned." Students understand $2.00 intuitively — dollar framing makes rewards tangible from session one.

GOALS Chunked Next Actions

3 weekly goals replace 329 scholarship listings. Each maps to a high-value action with a specific BP reward and a clear done state — reducing overwhelm and driving return visits.

ALERT Scheduled Return Trigger

The banner tells users exactly when to come back and why — "the 500 BP scholarship opens tomorrow." Appointment-viewing behavior without requiring a push notification.

TRUST Transparent Earning Model

"Vetted Partner Program" → "Partners pay to help fund your debt." A 3-step apply → earn → redeem model on every state makes the business model legible and builds trust.

MATCH Best Match, Not All Matches

One curated scholarship card per state with win-probability signals, deadline urgency, and effort required. Returning users see in-progress applications surfaced first.

PROOF Community Stats Anchor Trust

"$14,230 paid toward loans · 847 earned rewards · 23 winners this week" — three live-feeling stats that confirm real people are winning real money on the platform.

03 — Behavioral Impact

Why These Changes Improve Behavior

Each design decision maps to a specific psychological lever that reduces friction or builds confidence at the right moment in the user journey.

💵
Dollar Framing Collapses the Value Gap
Switching from BP to dollars in the hero card eliminates the translation step new users must do to understand what they're earning. "$2.00 toward your loans" is immediately meaningful. "267 BP" is not. This single change reduces the cognitive load of understanding the platform at the moment of highest drop-off risk.
🎯
Weekly Goals Create a Return Ritual
Users with a structured set of goals to complete have a reason to come back. The weekly reset mirrors familiar patterns from fitness apps and games — a fresh set of actions every Monday creates appointment behavior without requiring external triggers like email or push.
⏰
The Alert Banner Pre-Schedules the Return Visit
By naming a specific opportunity opening the next day, the banner turns a passive exit into an active commitment. Users who leave knowing "something opens tomorrow" are significantly more likely to return than users who leave with no pending reason to come back.
🛡️
Transparent Model Converts Skeptics
Gen Z carries structural skepticism about financial platforms. Making the revenue model explicit — "partners pay to help fund your debt" — transforms a source of distrust into a source of alignment. When students understand why Bold works the way it does, they engage with partner offers instead of avoiding them.
📱
Mobile CTA Unlocks the Highest-Revenue Channel
Mobile sessions generate 30–50% more revenue than web sessions, and push notifications are the most effective re-engagement channel available. A prominent download CTA on every dashboard state — framed as "earn more, faster" — directly converts web users to the higher-value surface.

Behavioral Signals

📋 Weekly Goals Completion Rate

Best leading indicator of D30 retention. Users who complete at least 2 of 3 weekly goals are significantly more likely to return the following week. Instrument this first.

⏰ Alert Banner Attribution

Track D1 return rate for users who saw the "come back tomorrow" banner vs. those who didn't. If the delta is significant, expand banner content to include more forward-scheduled hooks.

💵 Hero Card Dollar Engagement

Click-through rate on the hero card progress bar and milestone CTA. High engagement signals the dollar framing is landing. Low engagement signals the milestone target needs to feel closer.

🤝 Partner Offer Completion Rate

Direct revenue signal. If the transparent earning model is working, partner offer completion should increase without any change to the offers themselves — purely from the framing shift.
